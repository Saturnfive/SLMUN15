Security Council
Under the UN Charter, the Security Council has primary responsibility for the maintenance of international peace and security. It is the only UN body with the authority to issue legally binding resolutions to member states while other committees can only make recommendations. Its powers include the establishment of peacekeeping operations, the establishment of international sanctions and the authorization of military action through Security Council resolutions. The Council is made up of 15 nations: five are permanent members and the remaining 10 seats rotate every 2 years among the nations in the UN. The delegates of the Security Council of SLMUN 2015 are expected to have a wide knowledge about international peace and security, disarmament, social and humanitarian issues as well as economic issues. This wide range of knowledge will be crucial for the formulation and passing of resolutions within the council.
