Special Summit on Terrorism
SLMUN 2015 has a desire to go above and beyond the constraints of the UN system. The constraints have caused a deadlock that needs to be broken if progress is to be achieved. Thus, we are introducing a new and intriguing committee. The SSoT is a Head of State summit dedicated to the issue of Terrorism. It was inspired by the series of international summits held recently on the issue of Islamic State. Terrorism may still appear to be a security issue that reminds one of the 9/11 attacks. Yet it is not just another security issue that can be dealt with merely through military means. Under the façade of violence, terrorism has a soft underbelly of various social issues that have exacerbated in a globalizing world. The root causes of terrorism including poverty, unemployment and extremism have been identified but have not been efficiently curbed. The Security Council already has a packed agenda and thus we needed a new space which has an extra capacity for action. A head of state summit is not merely about being a delegate. It is about being a leader and emulating the people you represent. On your shoulders falls the responsibility of executive power and thus the power to change the world through executive consensus. It would have been lovely to have all UN member state being part of this Summit, yet it is infeasible if we are to find substantive solutions to this terror scourge upon civilization. Therefore the Summit will only feature 40 Heads of State.
