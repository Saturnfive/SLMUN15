UNHRC
United Nations Human Rights Council
The United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) is an interdepartmental body within the UN consisting of 47 different member states. Topics discussed by the UNHRC include specific Human Rights situations involving specific countries but have an affect the majority of member states, groups of people and certain individuals. The goal of the UNHRC is to promote universality, interdependence, inherent cooperation, to become a transparent intergovernmental process and to be realistic. The focus of the UNHRC lies not only with the actions of Member States but also on Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) and national Human Rights institutions. UNHRC’s method of work focuses on three major dimensions which involve standard-setting, monitoring, and implementation on the ground. This is done by providing the best level of expertise and substantive support to the different United Nations Human Rights bodies.
