UNFCCC
United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change
Climate change is an intricate problem of a very sensitive nature. Although it is of environmental causes, Climate Change has consequences affecting all spheres of existence on our planet earth. It has great impact in terms of many global issues ranging from poverty to economic development to population growth to sustainable development and resource management. In recognizing this problem, countries took a joint initiative and formed an international treaty; The United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. Since its establishment in 1992, UNFCCC has worked towards global initiatives to combat climate change and minimize effects of any inevitable impacts. Towards achieving this goal many Global Action Plans, Conferences and Global initiatives had been established. Thus it is the Parent treaty to the Kyoto Protocol, Bali Road Map, Cancun Agreements, Durban Outcomes, Doha Climate summit and Warsaw Outcomes. In this sense it is also one of the few Treaties having the capability to lead the entire world to work for a common goal and is accepted by 196 member states coming as close to universal membership as there ever could be.
“With so much evidence of depleting natural resources, toxic waste, climate change, irreparable harm to our food chain and rapidly increasing instances of natural disasters, why do we keep perpetuating the problem? Why do we continue marching at the same alarming beat ? ” ~Yehuda Berg~
